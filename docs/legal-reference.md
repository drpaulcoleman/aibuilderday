# Utah Civil Commitment Statutes — Legal Reference

Source: Utah Code Title 26B, Chapter 5, Part 3 (Effective 5/6/2026)

---

## 26B-5-301 — Definitions

Key definitions for the system:

- **Substantial Danger** — Due to mental illness, an individual is at serious risk of:
  (a) suicide; (b) serious bodily self-injury; (c) serious bodily injury due to inability to provide basic necessities (food, clothing, shelter); (d) causing/attempting serious bodily injury to another; (e) engaging in harmful sexual conduct; or (f) if not treated, suffering severe distress associated with significant impairment of judgment/reason/behavior causing substantial deterioration of ability to function independently.

- **Mental Illness** — A psychiatric disorder that substantially impairs an individual's mental, emotional, behavioral, or related functioning (or as defined by DSM/ICD).

- **Designated Examiner** — (a) Licensed physician (preferably psychiatrist) designated by the division as specially qualified; or (b) Licensed mental health professional designated by division with 5+ years continual experience treating mental illness.

- **Mental Health Officer** — Individual designated by a local mental health authority, qualified by training/experience in recognition of mental illness, to: (a) apply for/provide certification for temporary commitment; or (b) assist in transportation arrangement.

- **Local Mental Health Authority (LMHA)** — Governs the mental health catchment area where the adult resides or is found.

- **Patient** — Individual under commitment to custody or treatment services of an LMHA, or undergoing essential treatment.

- **Serious Bodily Injury** — Bodily injury involving substantial risk of death, unconsciousness, extreme physical pain, protracted disfigurement, or protracted loss/impairment of function.

## 26B-5-331 — Temporary Commitment (Emergency Hold — Blue/Pink Sheet)

### Two Pathways to Temporary Commitment:

**Pathway (1)(a) — Written Application + Medical Certification (Blue Sheet / Form 34-1):**
1. Written application by a responsible individual stating belief that adult, due to mental illness, poses substantial danger to self/others
2. PLUS certification by licensed physician, PA, NP, or designated examiner stating:
   - Examined the adult within 3-day period preceding certification
   - Opinion that adult poses substantial danger due to mental illness

**Pathway (1)(b) — Peace Officer / Mental Health Officer Observation (Pink Sheet / Form 34-2):**
1. Officer observes conduct giving probable cause to believe:
   - Adult has mental illness AND
   - Because of illness and conduct, adult poses substantial danger
2. Officer completes temporary commitment application including:
   - Prescribed form from the division
   - Statement of belief of substantial danger
   - Specific nature of the danger
   - Summary of observations
   - Statement of facts that called adult to attention

### 72-Hour Rule (Subsection 3):
- Maximum hold: **72 hours after commitment, excluding Saturdays, Sundays, and state holidays**
- At expiration, patient SHALL be released UNLESS:
  - (a) Application for involuntary commitment under 26B-5-332 is commenced (may include order of detention); OR
  - (b) Patient makes a voluntary application for admission

### Release Requirements (Subsection 2):
- If patient no longer meets commitment criteria, LMHA designee SHALL:
  - Document the change and release the patient
  - If admitted under officer pathway (1)(b), notify LMHA of release if deemed appropriate by licensed provider or patient consents

### Transport (Subsection 4):
- Adult taken into peace officer protective custody by reasonable means if necessary for public safety
- Transported to LMHA-designated facility by: ambulance, law enforcement (city/municipal or county sheriff), or nonemergency secured behavioral health transport

### Officer Discretion (Subsection 5):
- Officer MAY exercise discretion NOT to take into custody/transport if probable cause to believe it would increase risk of substantial danger
- Must document details and circumstances in report

### Patient Rights (Subsection 6):
- Must be informed of reason for commitment
- Within 3 hours of arrival: right to make a phone call (at LMHA expense)
- Right to see and communicate with an attorney

### Discharge Requirements (Subsection 8):
- LMHA must provide discharge instructions including:
  - Safety plan
  - Primary care provider notification
  - Referral to services if discharged without food/housing/economic security
  - Crisis hotline number
  - Psychiatric advance directive info
  - Medication change information
  - How to contact LMHA
  - How to request medical records
- If medications changed: clinically appropriate supply of medications at discharge
- Paper or electronic format (patient preference)
- Must document refusal if patient refuses instructions
- LMHA shall attempt follow-up at least 48 hours after discharge

## 26B-5-332 — Involuntary Commitment Under Court Order (White Sheet)

### Application (Subsection 1):
- Filed by responsible individual with credible knowledge
- Filed in court of county where proposed patient resides or is found
- Must include:
  - Patient name, DOB, SSN (unless not reasonably available)
  - Certificate from physician/designated examiner (examined within 7-day period) OR sworn written statement that patient refused examination
  - Statement whether patient has previously been under assisted outpatient treatment

### Pre-Hearing (Subsection 2-3):
- Court requires applicant to consult with appropriate LMHA before or at hearing
- Court MAY issue order to immediately place patient in LMHA custody for examination if:
  - Reasonable basis to believe mental illness posing danger requiring commitment; OR
  - Patient refuses interview/voluntary treatment

### Order of Detention (Subsection 4):
- Court provides notice of commencement to proposed patient before/upon placement
- Place of detention maintains copy of order

### Notice Requirements (Subsection 5):
- Notice to: applicant, legal guardian, immediate adult family members, legal counsel, LMHA, and any designated persons
- Advises that hearing may be held within time provided by law

### Designated Examiners (Subsection 8):
- Court appoints TWO designated examiners within 24 hours (excluding weekends/holidays) of judicial order
- Must not have signed the application or certification
- One must be: licensed physician; or psychiatric NP/CNS (nationally certified, doctorally trained, 2+ years inpatient experience)
- One may be designated by patient or patient's counsel

### Hearing Timeline (Subsection 9):
- **Hearing within 10 calendar days** after examiners appointed

### Examination Process (Subsection 10):
- Examiners examine separately
- Conducted via telehealth unless examiner determines in-person needed
- Must inform unrepresented patient: right not to speak, nature/reason of exam, court-ordered, statements may support commitment, findings go to court
- Report to court within 24 hours: mentally ill? agreed to voluntary? acceptable alternatives?

### Commitment Criteria (Subsection 16):
Court orders commitment if **clear and convincing evidence** of ALL:
1. Proposed patient has mental illness
2. Due to illness, poses substantial danger to self/others
3. Lacks ability to engage in rational decision-making regarding treatment
4. No appropriate less-restrictive alternative
5. LMHA can provide adequate and appropriate treatment

### Commitment Duration (Subsection 17):
- Treatment period may NOT exceed **6 months** without review hearing
- Upon review: may be for **indeterminate period** if criteria will last indeterminately
- Court maintains list of all patients under commitment orders
- **At least 2 weeks before expiration**, court informs LMHA
- LMHA immediately reexamines; discharges if criteria no longer met; otherwise proceeds to new hearing
- Indeterminate commitments: LMHA reexamines at **6-month intervals**

### Patient Rights at Hearing (Subsection 14-15):
- Right to counsel (appointed if needed, county pays for indigent)
- Right to appear, testify, present witnesses, cross-examine
- Hearing conducted informally but preserving due process
- Hearing conducted remotely unless good cause for in-person

### Discharge Instructions (Subsection 20):
- Same comprehensive discharge instruction requirements as 26B-5-331(8)

## Key Timelines for System Design

| Event | Timeline | System Implication |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| Emergency hold (Blue/Pink) | Max 72 hrs (excl. weekends/holidays) | Countdown must exclude Sat/Sun/state holidays |
| Patient phone call right | Within 3 hours of arrival | Alert/task to facility staff |
| Involuntary commitment application | Before 72-hr expiry | Escalation alerts at 48/60/68 hrs |
| Examiners appointed | Within 24 hrs of judicial order | Track court order → examiner assignment |
| Examiner report to court | Within 24 hrs of examination | Track examination status |
| Court hearing | Within 10 calendar days of examiner appointment | Track hearing date |
| Commitment order max duration | 6 months (initial) | Set renewal date |
| Court notice to LMHA before expiry | At least 2 weeks before | Track renewal/review hearings |
| Indeterminate commitment review | Every 6 months | Recurring review schedule |
| Post-discharge follow-up | At least 48 hours after | Follow-up task generation |

---

## Key Compliance Requirements for Audit

1. Every status change must be auditable (who, when, what)
2. 72-hour clock must properly exclude weekends and state holidays
3. Patient must be informed of commitment reason (document this)
4. Phone call right within 3 hours must be tracked
5. Discharge instructions must be provided and documented (including refusals)
6. Medication supply at discharge if meds were changed
7. Follow-up attempt at 48 hours post-discharge
8. Court notifications at 2-week pre-expiry
9. 6-month reexamination cycle for indeterminate commitments
10. All examiner reports within 24 hours of examination
