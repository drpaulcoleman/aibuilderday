**Slide 1: Guardian AI: Protecting Rights, Preventing Gaps** *(Suggested Time: 45 seconds)* **Speaker Notes:** "\[Smile, warm and energetic tone\] Hi everyone, thank you so much for your time and for being part of this build challenge. Before we talk about technology, I want to ground us in why this matters. This past September, my family navigated a really tough moment when my son experienced a mental health crisis. We were incredibly fortunate; the local police intervened and supported our family with such compassion and care. It opened my eyes to how critical this process is—and how the systems supporting our first responders and caseworkers need to be as supportive as they are. That is why I am so energized to introduce this hackathon challenge: building a Digital Commitment Management prototype we call 'Guardian AI'. Your goal is simple: protect rights, and prevent gaps."

**Slide 2: The Crisis: Why the 72-Hour Clock Matters** *(Suggested Time: 30 seconds)* **Speaker Notes:** "Imagine it is 2 AM in Provo. A police officer, much like the ones who helped my son, responds to a crisis and files a 'Blue Sheet' for an emergency commitment. The moment that paper is signed, a critical 72-hour legal clock starts ticking. But here is the reality: today in Utah, that life-or-death clock is often tracked on a manual spreadsheet. If a court order isn't received in time, the individual must be released regardless of their condition, creating a massive legal liability and a serious risk to public safety."

**Slide 3: The Problem: Disconnected Data & Paper Silos** *(Suggested Time: 30 seconds)* **Speaker Notes:** "The root of the problem is a highly fragmented system. Emergency sheets often start as physical paper forms in the field, taking days to get entered centrally. Worse, the data is disconnected. The Utah Court System doesn't automatically notify Health and Human Services when a judge signs a commitment order. Because Local Mental Health Authorities run separate systems that don't talk to each other, we see missed deadlines and potential civil rights violations."

**Slide 4: The Solution: Proactive Commitment Management** *(Suggested Time: 35 seconds)* **Speaker Notes:** "\[Energetic transition\] This is where you come in. We need you to build the Guardian AI prototype. At its core, we are looking for a unified system of record that captures those emergency Blue Sheets the exact moment they are filed in the field. We also need you to bridge the disconnected data gap. Your solution needs to instantly grab signed 'White Sheets' from the Court System and push them directly into the patient's case record in real-time."

**Slide 5: Intelligence in Action: Autonomous Monitoring** *(Suggested Time: 35 seconds)* **Speaker Notes:** "But we don't just want a database; we want intelligence in action. Your prototype must actively watch the data. We are challenging you to build 24/7 autonomous monitoring for every single commitment clock. The system needs to act as a digital safety net—proactively alerting a caseworker right at the 48-hour mark, and automatically escalating to a supervisor at 68 hours if no court order is found. Nobody falls through the cracks."

**Slide 6: The Demo: Real-Time Field to Court Workflows** *(Suggested Time: 40 seconds)* **Speaker Notes:** "For your demo, we want to see highly usable interfaces: a mobile-optimized view for the officer in the field to file a Blue Sheet in under two minutes, and a caseworker dashboard featuring a dominant, live countdown visual. We highly encourage you to 'vibe code' this solution using any AI coding tool of your choice to move fast and creatively. You are not required to build on a specific stack—we just want to see your most innovative approach to solving this workflow."

**Slide 7: The Impact: Compliance, Safety, and Dignity** *(Suggested Time: 35 seconds)* **Speaker Notes:** "\[Ground your tone, speak warmly\] The impact your builds will have for Utah is profound. By automating compliance, you eliminate state liability by ensuring no one is held past their legal window. You get the state ready for upcoming legislative mandates. You dramatically improve field safety by giving officers active commitment statuses on their phones in seconds. But most importantly, you ensure continuity of care and protect the dignity and civil rights of individuals in crisis."
